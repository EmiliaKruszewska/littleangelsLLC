PCA - Personal Care Assistant

Personal care assistants assists elderly, recovering, or disabled clients with the activities of daily living. Offers support and companionship to clients. Helps clients handle day-to-day self-care. Communicates with families of clients to apprise them of the client's condition and progress. Advises clients and families on nutrition, hygiene, household activities, and other appropriate areas. Work in care facilities or private homes to help clients with common tasks, for example:

  • bathing,
  • grooming,
  • dressing,
  • using toilet facilities,
  • house cleaning,
  • shopping,
  • cooking...
  • AND SO MUCH MORE!

We also ensure clients receive their medication on time, support clients who can't move on their own and provide emotional support.

Personal Care Assistant Job Duties:

  • Provides support, encouragement, and companionship by engaging clients in activities, such as playing games, visiting places, taking walks, or having conversations
  • Engages client in muscle stretching and strengthening exercises as needed to improve range of motion
  • Helps client with self-care, such as toileting, bathing, dressing, brushing teeth, etc.
  • Assists clients with household finances, such as depositing checks, paying bills, and managing money
  • Cares for clients with physical, emotional, or cognitive (e.g., Alzheimer's) impairments
  • Handles household chores for clients, such as laundry, dishes, changing sheets, and other basic cleaning
  • Speaks with family to arrange schedule and activities and stay apprised of client's well-being and needs
  • Dispenses and administers medications as directed by medical providers and family members
  • Assists clients with ambulation and mobility as needed, transporting from one location to another with a wheelchair or other form of support
